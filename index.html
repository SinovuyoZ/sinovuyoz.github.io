The Model

One question.
Six connected areas.

Not a sequence. A system. Select any area to see what it shapes.

Select any area above
Growth problems rarely live in one place. Each area shapes the others. The issue is usually in the connection between them.
Customer behaviour influences visibility, activation, partnerships and commercial performance
What customers actually do, versus what a model assumes. Most growth plans are built on the second and fail on the first. That gap distorts everything downstream.
Visibility influences customer behaviour, activation rates and commercial performance
A strong product with weak commercial architecture is invisible. Visibility is not awareness. It is whether the right customer finds the right offer in the right context. Get this wrong and activation never gets a fair test.
Activation influences behaviour, visibility, revenue and overall commercial complexity
Revenue is an output. The work is identifying which stage, acquisition, activation or retention, is quietly broken, and fixing that stage rather than rebuilding everything around it.
Revenue performance influences activation priorities, partnership decisions and complexity
Revenue performance determines which partnerships get attention and resources, and which activation problems get treated as urgent versus theoretical.
Partnership quality influences customer behaviour, revenue outcomes and operational complexity
Relationships compound or they do not, depending on whether the structure rewards mutual outcomes, not just transaction volume. A misaligned partnership reshapes customer behaviour too.
Complexity touches every other area
In regulated or multi-market environments, execution fails without alignment first. Knowing who needs to move, and in what order, is itself the deliverable.
The Pattern
The same gap, every time: the seam between teams. Each function's metrics look fine. The customer experiences the break.
Where This Shows Up

Six places growth
quietly breaks.

Each looks isolated from inside its own function. They are usually the same gap in different forms.

Customer Activation
Looks Like
A sign-up that never becomes a funded, active customer. Often attributed to low intent.
Usually Is
Friction at a specific step nobody owns, sitting between marketing's promise and product's delivery.
Revenue Leakage
Looks Like
Acquisition numbers are healthy. Revenue is not growing at the same rate.
Usually Is
One stage of the journey, invisible in top-line reporting, losing most of the value.
Strategic Partnerships
Looks Like
A partnership that exists on paper but never delivers expected results.
Usually Is
No shared accountability. Both sides assumed the other would drive it.
GTM Execution
Looks Like
A strategy that sounded right in the room. Does not show up in the market.
Usually Is
A translation gap between what leadership decided and what the front line does differently.
Commercial Visibility
Looks Like
Strong product. Flat growth. A team convinced the answer is more marketing spend.
Usually Is
The right customer cannot find or recognise the offer in the context they are in.
Cross-Functional Alignment
Looks Like
Every team shows a dashboard that says things are fine.
Usually Is
Each dashboard measures something slightly different. None of them is the customer's experience.
