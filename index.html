Growth Is Rarely An Acquisition Problem

Businesses don’t usually struggle because they lack activity. Customers arrive. Partners generate traffic. Campaigns create engagement. The challenge is often understanding what happens next. Where is value being created? Where is momentum being lost? Which activities are producing meaningful commercial outcomes?

My work has always centred around creating that clarity. Because growth becomes easier when organisations can clearly see what deserves attention.

Case Studies

Improving Visibility Across a Trading Platform

Connecting fragmented behavioral data to account funding milestones to clarify channel value.

+20% Funded Conversion | +15% Customer Activation
+

Business Reality

The business was growing, but confidence wasn’t. Customers were registering. Partners were generating traffic. Campaigns were producing activity. Yet leadership still struggled to answer:
• Which acquisition channels created long-term value?
• Which customer behaviours predicted funded accounts?
• Which partners generated revenue rather than volume?
• Where was growth actually being lost?
Every team had data. Nobody had a complete picture.

What Was Missing

Leadership could see registrations. They could see traffic. They could see activity. What they couldn’t see was which actions actually led to funded accounts and long-term value.

What I Focused On

I mapped the customer journey, updated how we look at user actions in the CRM, and connected those day-to-day behaviours directly to actual account funding and financial milestones.

Outcomes

  • +20% Increase in Funded Conversion
  • +15% Increase in Customer Activation
  • Improved visibility into customer behaviour
  • Stronger understanding of partner performance
  • Better alignment across teams
  • More confident commercial decision-making

Key Learning

The biggest growth constraint is often not acquisition. It’s understanding what creates value after acquisition.

Building Visibility Across a Partner Ecosystem

Building technical tracking systems to pivot partner networks from volume-based metrics to true bottom-line yield.

End-to-End Funnel Tracking | Full Source Lifecycle Visibility
+

Business Reality

Partner programmes are often measured by activity. Traffic. Clicks. Registrations. Introductions. The problem is that activity doesn’t always create commercial value. As partner networks expanded, the business faced a growing challenge: There was no consistent way to understand which relationships were creating meaningful outcomes. Some partners generated significant traffic but limited long-term value. Others delivered fewer customers but stronger commercial performance. Decision-making increasingly relied on assumptions rather than visibility.

What Was Missing

The ability to link performance back to specific partners, which meant the business was consistently rewarding raw traffic volume over real value.

What I Focused On

I built the tracking infrastructure needed to follow partner traffic through every phase of the business funnel, giving leadership a clear view of actual financial returns.

Outcomes

  • Eliminated guesswork in partner tracking
  • Stronger understanding of partner performance
  • Improved partner accountability
  • Better alignment across teams
  • More confident commercial decision-making

Key Learning

Understanding which customers create value is far more powerful than simply counting how many arrive.

Building Without A Playbook

The environments that shaped me were rarely mature, predictable or fully built. Whether launching new initiatives inside global brands, supporting growth in fintech businesses or helping teams navigate change, I’ve often worked where there wasn’t a clear roadmap. That’s where I do my best work.

I’ve spent much of my career working across teams, functions and objectives. Not because I set out to become cross-functional, but because the challenges themselves demanded it. Many of the opportunities I’ve worked on required building new processes, testing new approaches and creating momentum with limited resources. Those experiences taught me how product, marketing, partnerships, operations and customer behaviour influence one another. Most importantly, they taught me how to create clarity when the path forward isn’t obvious.

Why Companies Work With Me

Most business problems don’t fit neatly into a single department. Marketing sees one thing. Operations sees another. Commercial teams focus somewhere else. My experience developed across these environments, helping teams understand how customer behaviour, partnerships, systems and business outcomes influence one another. That perspective helps me spot opportunities that are often missed when teams work in isolation.
